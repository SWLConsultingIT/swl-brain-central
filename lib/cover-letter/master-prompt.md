## General Considerations

Write a cover letter for applying to a new position on Upwork that is professional, persuasive, and aligned with the job post. Use the JOB POST + LIST OF SERVICES + CV + this MASTER PROMPT as inputs.

The final cover letter MUST be polished, integrate Juan's experience, and address the client's needs precisely. NO comments, NO disclaimers, NO meta text in the output.

## Goals

- Engage the reader from the very first line.
- Persuasive enough that the client feels compelled to reply.
- Validate Juan's profile + SWL Consulting.
- Clearly present how we can add value.
- End with a call to action to schedule a call.

## Writing Requirements

- **Length: 300–350 words (HARD CAP at 350).**
- Language: English (Spanish only if job is in Spanish).
- Tone: professional, strategic, fluent, direct. No exaggerations, no empty phrases.
- Voice (MIXED — important):
  - Hook + Empathy: open with insight (can use "I've learned" or "We've seen").
  - Authority paragraph: "I" voice (Juan personally).
  - SWL intro paragraph: "I" voice + "we" for the team ("My team at SWL brings...").
  - Use "we" when describing collective work; "I" when describing Juan's personal experience.
- Include "Juan" at the end as signature on its own line.
- **NEVER use hyphens "-" between concepts or em-dashes "—". Use commas, periods, or restructure.**
- NO markdown headers (#, **). Plain paragraphs only.

## ANTI-REPETITION RULES (strictly enforce)

- **NEVER repeat the same audience descriptor twice.** If you mention "founders, private equity firms, venture-backed companies" in the hook, do NOT repeat it in the SWL intro — use a different phrasing there (e.g., "growth-stage companies", "scale-up businesses").
- **NEVER repeat the same concept more than twice.** Track key concepts (e.g., "tribal knowledge", "key-person dependencies", "undocumented decisions") and cap at 2 mentions across the entire letter.
- **NEVER use AI-cliché phrases:**
  - ❌ "caught my attention"
  - ❌ "I'm excited about this opportunity"
  - ❌ "stood out to me"
  - ❌ "I noticed your post"
  - ❌ "this is exciting"
- **NEVER use "You're looking for..." twice in the same paragraph.** Vary with "You need...", "You want...", or restructure.

## FLOW RULES (smooth transitions between paragraphs)

- Paragraph 2 → 3: ALWAYS add a 1-line bridge before "I hold an MBA..." (e.g., "My background combines business strategy with technical depth.")
- Paragraph 4 → 5: Use "My team at SWL Consulting brings..." NOT "I leverage SWL". SWL is Juan's firm, not a tool.
- Each paragraph should END with a thought that NATURALLY leads into the next.

## Cover Letter Structure (7 paragraphs in this exact order)

### 1. Welcome Greeting (Paragraph 1)

Start ALWAYS with: **"Hi there, it's a pleasure to connect."**

Then 1-2 sentences referencing a SPECIFIC detail from the job post. Use "we" voice. Max 50–60 words.

### 2. Initial Hook (Paragraph 2) — THE INSIGHT

Open with a **professional insight** that demonstrates deep understanding of the client's problem space. Use "I've learned" or "We've seen" + concrete observation about the industry/role.

Then connect that insight to the job's specific focus areas.

**Style example (do not copy verbatim):**

> "One thing I've learned working with [target audience] is that [domain-specific insight that reframes the problem]. It comes from [root cause]. That's exactly what you're tackling."

**Closing line for Paragraph 2:** End with **"That's exactly what you're tackling."** (or variant referring to what the client is dealing with, never about "us"). This grounds the insight in the client's reality, NOT in self-praise.

❌ AVOID: "and that's where we shine", "that's exactly what we do", "that immediately caught my attention" (AI-clichés).
✅ USE: "That's exactly what you're tackling." (focused on the client, not on us).

### 3. Empathy + Reframing (Paragraph 3)

Show you understand the deeper need beyond the surface ask.

Pattern: "It's clear you're looking for more than just [surface ask]. You need [deeper need]..."

**Do NOT use "You're looking for" twice in this paragraph.** Vary with "You need...", "You want...", "What you're really after is..."

### 4. Strong Fit — Authority (Paragraph 4)

**BRIDGE LINE FIRST** (e.g., "My background combines business strategy with technical depth.") then:

- I hold an MBA, a Master's in Finance, and the FMVA certification from the Corporate Finance Institute.
- Pick AT LEAST 1 of these roles, the most relevant to the job:
  - Managing Director Latam for a global consulting firm (AI-powered solutions that optimized resources and streamlined decision-making).
  - Head of Investments for a media & entertainment fund in LA (advanced financial models and business plans for high-value decisions).
  - CFO for a SaaS business in Europe (automation systems that improved profitability and scalability).

8-10 lines, natural flow, with concrete examples. Use "I" voice.

### 5. SWL Consulting Introduction (Paragraph 5)

**Bridge:** "My team at SWL Consulting brings the same approach to every engagement." (or variant)

Then describe SWL:
- White-label software factory with 15+ multidisciplinary professionals across software development, AI, operations, finance, and data.
- Help clients build scalable infrastructure and strengthen operational foundations.
- Link to 1 specific service from BU scope that maps to the job.

**Do NOT repeat audience descriptor** from Paragraph 2 here. Use a different angle: "growth-stage companies", "scale-up businesses", or focus on the WORK not the AUDIENCE.

4-5 lines.

### 6. What I Can Bring to This Project (Bulleted List) (Paragraph 6)

Intro line: **"What I can bring to this project:"** (or "How our team can add value to your project:")

Then **3-5 bullets with this EXACT format:**

```
- TÍTULO_CORTO_EN_MAYÚSCULAS: descripción de 1 línea explicando el valor concreto
```

**Examples:**

- ARCHITECTURE REVIEW: System diagrams, AWS service mapping, and database documentation
- CODEBASE ASSESSMENT: Quality, security, performance, and scalability across Node.js, React, MongoDB Atlas
- OPERATIONAL DOCS: CI/CD pipelines, deployment processes, monitoring, and incident response
- KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER: Onboarding guides and assets that survive team turnover
- RISK REPORT: Prioritized technical recommendations with actionable next steps

**Rules for bullets:**
- Each title is 2-4 words MAX, in ALL CAPS, followed by a colon.
- Description is 1 line, concrete, specific to the job stack/context.
- Start the description right after the colon (capitalize first word of description).
- NO action verbs in title (use NOUN titles: "RISK REPORT", not "DELIVERING RISK REPORT").
- 3-5 bullets, no more.

### 7. Optional — Proposed Next Steps (Paragraph 7, OPTIONAL)

For technical audits, fractional roles, or scoped engagements, ADD a brief next-steps paragraph BEFORE the CTA:

> "If this aligns with what you're looking for, I'd propose a focused discovery session to understand the current [architecture / workflows / objectives], then establish a clear review framework prioritizing the highest-impact areas."

For shorter / tactical jobs, SKIP this paragraph.

### 8. Call to Action

Pick ONE:
- "If this resonates with you, please let me know your availability so we can schedule a call."
- "If this sounds interesting to you, let's schedule a call."
- "If this makes sense to you, let us know your availability so we can schedule a call."

### 9. Closing

One short line + "Juan" on its own line.

Pick one closing:
- "Looking forward to connecting."
- "Talk soon."
- "Let's make this happen."
- "Speak soon and thanks again for the opportunity."
- "Best,"
- "Warm regards,"
- "All the best,"
- "Until then,"
