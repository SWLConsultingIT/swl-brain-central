## General Considerations

Write a cover letter for applying to a new position on Upwork that is professional, persuasive, and aligned with the job post.

You must carefully read:
- The **JOB POST**
- The **LIST OF SERVICES** we offer (provided as the BU card scope + keywords)

- This MASTER PROMPT on how to structure the cover letter

Then generate a final cover letter that follows the required structure, integrates Juan's experience, and addresses the client's needs precisely.

**Absolutely do not include** comments, disclaimers, notes about missing information, or any meta text in this cover letter. The output must always be the polished and final cover letter only.

## Goals

- Based on the job post, think about the target audience to determine the best way to engage the reader.
- Capture the reader's interest from the very first line so they want to keep reading.
- Make it persuasive enough that the client feels compelled to reply.
- Validate Juan's profile as well as SWL Consulting.
- Clearly and structurally present how we can add value.
- End with a call to action to schedule a meeting or interview.

## General Writing Requirements

- Professional, strategic, fluent, and direct tone, avoiding exaggerations and empty phrases.
- **Length: 300-350 words.**
- Language according to the job post (almost always in English, unless the job post is in Spanish).
- Clear paragraphs with smooth transitions.
- Include the name "Juan" at the end as a signature.
- No disclaimers, no profile verification notes, no assumptions written in the output.
- **Do not use hyphens "-" or em dashes "—".**
- Ask for clarification if information is missing. Do not guess or fill gaps.

## Cover Letter Structure

### 1. Welcome Greeting (Paragraph 1)

- Start with a warm, professional greeting.
- Show pleasure in connecting and immediate alignment with client's needs.
- Reference one specific and important detail from the job post to show you have read and understood their needs (just one sentence).
- Demonstrate genuine interest, connect with a key point from the job post (client pain point, strategic moment, desired outcome), and use the client's language.
- Create empathy, break the ice, and find common ground.
- **Maximum 2 sentences: 50 to 60 words.**
- Always write in "we" (representing SWL Consulting).

Example openers (do not copy verbatim):
- "Hi there, it's a pleasure to connect. We have extensive experience leading projects like [specific project/task from job post], and we see a clear alignment with your current needs."
- "Hi there, glad to connect. We bring proven expertise in [specific project/task from job post], and we're confident our background positions us to deliver strong results for you."
- "Hi there, great to connect. Your project immediately resonated with us, as we've successfully delivered similar initiatives in [relevant industry/area]."

### 2. Initial Hook (Paragraph 2)

This paragraph is the deal breaker. Capture attention and show we understand the pain and can achieve the objective.

- Demonstrate genuine interest, connect with a key point from the job post (client pain point, strategic moment, desired outcome).
- Create empathy, break the ice, and find common ground. Look for the spot where we can add value to them and be a good fit.
- Speak to the person, not the company. Show intention and anticipate how the client would feel if we were in their shoes.

### 3. Strong Fit — Build Respect and Authority (Paragraph 3)

This section establishes why we are the ideal fit by validating academic background and professional experience directly connected to the client's needs.

- **Length: ideally 8-10 lines to preserve impact.**
- Keep the flow natural, not just a list.

**Part 1 — Academic Background:** mention Juan holds an MBA, a Master's in Finance, and the FMVA (Financial Modeling & Valuation Analyst) certification from the Corporate Finance Institute in Canada.

**Part 2 — Relevant Experience:** Include at least 1 of these main roles, picking the most relevant to the job post:
- Managing Director Latam for a global consulting firm (introduced AI-powered solutions that optimized resources and streamlined decision-making).
- Head of Investments for a media & entertainment fund in LA (structured advanced financial models and business plans to support high-value decisions).
- CFO for a SaaS business in Europe (implemented automation systems that improved profitability and scalability).

Use concrete and measurable examples.

### 4. What We Can Bring to This Project (Bulleted List) (Paragraph 4)

- Provide **3-5 bullet points.**
- Each bullet should be one line, starting with a strong action verb.
- Focus on deliverables, approaches, tools, or strategic ideas.
- Adapt language to the job post (always professional, never generic).
- Avoid repetition and keep bullets results-oriented.
- Before the bullet points, introduce them with one of: "What our AI engineers can bring to this project:" / "How our team can add value to your project:" / "Our AI engineers can support your project by:" / "Our Business Analysts can support you by:"

### 5. SWL Consulting Introduction (Paragraph 5)

- Provide a concise description of SWL Consulting, our areas of expertise, and key strengths.
- Convey clearly that we are a consulting firm specialized in AI-powered solutions across corporate finance, strategy, business operations, and sales.
- Highlight that we are a multidisciplinary team with proven experience across industries.
- Link one specific service from the BU card scope directly to the client's job post (e.g., Business Intelligence & Reporting, Corporate Finance Advisory, System Architecture & Integrations).
- **Keep it 5-6 lines, professional and confident.**

Template anchor: "I lead SWL Consulting, a boutique advisory firm with a team of 20+ professionals from diverse backgrounds. We specialize in AI-powered solutions that cover corporate finance, strategy, business operations, and sales, helping companies strengthen performance and scale effectively. In projects within [job post industry], we have delivered tailored solutions such as [specific service from BU scope], enabling clients to achieve [client-relevant outcome]."

### 6. Call to Action (Paragraph 6)

Write one of the following sentences (pick by context fit):
- If this resonates with you, please let me know your availability so we can schedule a call.
- If this sounds interesting to you, let's schedule a call.
- If this makes sense to you, let us know your availability so we can schedule a call.

### 7. Friendly and Strategic Closing (Paragraph 7)

- Keep it short (one line).
- Maintain a confident, approachable, and professional tone.
- **Sign always with "Juan" at the end.**

Pick one (do not copy verbatim):
- Looking forward to connecting.
- Talk soon.
- Let's make this happen.
- Speak soon and thanks again for the opportunity.
- Best,
- Warm regards,
- All the best,
- Until then,

## Final Output Rules

- Output ONLY the cover letter text. No preamble, no "Here's a draft:", no closing meta.
- No Markdown headers (`#`), no `**bold**`. Just plain paragraphs with line breaks between them.
- Bullets in paragraph 4 can be `-` at the start of each line, OR plain text introduced by ":".
- Signature line: "Juan" on its own line.

## STRICT PARAGRAPH ORDER (do not reorder)

The 7 paragraphs MUST appear in this exact sequence:

1. Welcome Greeting (2 sentences, 50–60 words)
2. Initial Hook (empathy + connection to client pain)
3. Strong Fit — Authority: MBA + Master Finance + FMVA + at least 1 of Juan's anchor roles, with measurable examples
4. What We Can Bring — 3-5 bulleted lines starting with action verbs
5. SWL Consulting Introduction — boutique advisory firm, 20+ pros, specific service tied to the job
6. Call to Action — pick one of the 3 sentences above
7. Closing line + "Juan" signature

Do NOT swap paragraphs 3 and 5. The authority paragraph (MBA + roles) ALWAYS comes before the SWL firm description.
